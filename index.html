How to use the monthly tracker
A quick guide to getting the most out of your tracker.
The three columns

Each month is organised into three columns. Add tasks to whichever column fits best — you can always move them later.

● In progressTasks you are actively working on right now. ● PlannedTasks you intend to start this month but haven't yet. ● BlockedTasks you cannot progress because something or someone is in the way. Use notes to explain the blocker.
Task buttons
✓Mark as completed — moves to the Completed section. ✗Mark as cancelled — for work dropped or outside your control. →Move to a different column — e.g. move a planned task to Blocked, or a blocked task back to In progress once the issue is resolved. ◇Mark as ongoing — the task automatically carries over to the next month without any manual action. The diamond turns solid (◆) when active. Use this for long-running work that spans several months or longer. +▲Add or edit a note. Notes support bullet points, strikethrough, date insertion, and a progress percentage. ↩Restore a completed or cancelled task back to its original column if you marked it in error. ✕Delete a task permanently. A toast notification appears with an Undo option for 10 seconds.
Editing task names

Double-click any task name to edit it inline. Press Enter to save your changes or Escape to cancel.

Notes and formatting

Click +▲ on any task to open the note editor. The toolbar provides four formatting options:

• ListInserts a bullet point at the cursor. Press Enter to continue the list on the next line. Press Enter on an empty bullet to end the list. SWraps selected text in ~~ for strikethrough. Carries through to the copied manager update as [done]. Click again on struck text to remove it. 📅Opens a calendar picker to insert a date. Dates are inserted in DD/MM/YY format (e.g. 14/03/26). Use arrow keys to navigate the calendar or press Enter to select a day. ProgressType a number between 0 and 100 in the Progress field to set a percentage. A colour-coded bar appears on the task card and the figure carries through to the manager update and PDF export. Setting 100% and confirming automatically marks the task as completed.

Press Cmd+Enter (Mac) or Ctrl+Enter (Windows) to save a note quickly without clicking the Save button.

Tags

Tags appear automatically on tasks to show their history at a glance.

carriedThis task was carried over from a previous month.
ongoingThis task carries over automatically each month until marked completed or cancelled.
Navigating months

Use the arrows at the top to move between months. Each month's tasks are stored separately so you can look back at any time.

If you have incomplete tasks from a previous month, a blue banner appears offering to carry them all over to the current month in one click. Tasks marked as ongoing carry over automatically whenever you navigate forward — no manual action needed.

Search

Use the search bar in the top right to search across all tasks and notes from every month. Results show the month they belong to and a preview of any matching note content.

Manager update

The manager update preview at the bottom of the page builds automatically as you add tasks. Click Copy update to copy the formatted text to your clipboard, ready to paste into an email, Teams message, or Slack. Progress percentages and note content are included in the copied text.

Exporting as PDF

Click Export PDF to generate a formatted report. You can export the current month only, all months with data, or choose specific months. A light or dark theme can be selected before generating. The PDF opens in a new tab — use your browser's print dialogue to save it as a file.

Clearing completed and cancelled tasks

Click Clear all on the Completed or Cancelled sections to remove all tasks from that section for the current month. A toast notification appears with an Undo option for 10 seconds in case you clear something by mistake.

Saving your data

Data saves automatically to your browser's local storage every time you make a change. As long as you use the same browser on the same device, your tasks will be there when you return.

Use Export JSON to download a full backup of all your data at any time. Use Import JSON to restore from a backup or move your data to a different device.

Import JSON
Paste a previously exported JSON file to restore your data. This will overwrite your current data.
